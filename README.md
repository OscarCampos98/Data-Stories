# Data Stories: War, Energy, and Geopolitics

This repository contains a weekend data analytics project built around article-style analysis and supporting data visualizations.

The goal is to combine data analysis, storytelling, and geopolitical context into readable analytical pieces for an informed general audience. Each story is organized as its own project folder with data, notebooks, charts, and a dedicated README documenting the process.

## Project Overview

This repo currently contains two data stories:

1. **4 Years of War — What the Data Actually Shows**
2. **The Electrostate: China's EV and Clean Energy Dominance**

Both stories use publicly available datasets and reports to explore major global shifts through data. The focus is not only on creating charts, but on explaining what the data means, why it matters, and how it connects to larger political, economic, and strategic trends.

## Story 1: 4 Years of War — What the Data Actually Shows

This story analyzes the Russia-Ukraine war through multiple data-driven angles, including the human cost, territorial change, economic damage, refugee displacement, and the globalization of the conflict.

Key themes include:

- Casualty trajectories on both sides of the war
- Russian territorial gains compared against the cost in lives and equipment
- Ukraine’s economic collapse, energy infrastructure damage, and rising poverty
- The refugee crisis and long-term displacement across Europe
- Foreign fighters and international involvement on both sides
- North Korean military support to Russia, including troops and artillery supply
- Coerced, trafficked, and economically motivated foreign fighters

This story aims to show the scale and complexity of the war beyond daily battlefield updates. It looks at the war as a long-term human, economic, military, and geopolitical event.

## Story 2: The Electrostate — China's EV and Clean Energy Dominance

This story examines how China became the dominant global power in electric vehicles, batteries, solar manufacturing, drones, robotics, and clean energy supply chains.

The central idea is that China is becoming the world’s first major “electrostate” — a country whose industrial power is built around the technologies expected to define the next century.

Key themes include:

- EV production and export growth by country
- Battery production and battery price trends
- Control over critical mineral supply chains
- Solar panel manufacturing dominance
- China’s position in drones, robotics, and clean technology
- US and EU tariffs on Chinese EVs
- Risks of retaliation and trade escalation
- What this means for Western automakers and geopolitical leverage

This story focuses on how industrial strategy, supply chains, technology, and trade policy are reshaping global power.

### Planned Data Sources

- BloombergNEF
- BNEF Battery Price Survey
- UN Comtrade
- Visual Capitalist supply chain data
- Additional public datasets and reporting where needed
